Actual or Real-life Applications of the 1st Quarter lessons

  • Lesson 1: XHTML
    • We can use XHTML when we want to create a website and we want our website to avoid any error, have stricter rules, and make sure that our code is well-organized.
    • XHTML works well with other XML features which we can apply to our website.
    • It is useful to learn XHTML since a lot of new devices and all new web browsers support xhtml because there came to a point where a huge amount of web pages is written in wrong syntax.
  • Lesson 2: Lists
    • Lists can be used in real-life situations like making a to-do list of the things we need to do. (e.g.,homework)
    • Lists can be used to create a grocery checklist.
    • List can be used to make a step-by-step instruction.
  • Lessons 3 & 4: Tables
    • Tables can be used to arrange a large amount of data so that it can be easily understand.
    • Tables can be used to create a schedule just like our schedule in school everyday.
    • Tables can be used to show results from surveys and polls. Businesses use tables to keep track of stock levels, and orders and supplies of their product.
  • Lesson 5: Links
    • Links can be used in academic papers, educational platforms, research databases and more (e.g., When you want to list all your references in your research plan in a bibliography, you can use links that can be put in citations).
    • Links can be used in social media platforms (e.g., When you want to share a meme to your friend).
    • Links can be used in digital marketing, links can direct potential customers to landing pages or special offers from their products and services, allowing companies to track their possible customers.
    • Links can be used in communication, links in emails allows recipients to easily access websites, documents and resources.
    • Links is also very useful in creating websites as it can help as to have a better web navigation.
  • Lessons 6, 7 & 8: Forms
    • Forms can be used to take someone's private information (legally) through printed forms or online forms.
    • Forms can be used for registering students in schools or educational programs, it can also be used in gather feedbacks from student on courses they take or teaching quality.
    • It can also be used as job applications forms and employee timesheets (to track hours worked and attendance).
    • Governments also use forms, for example the census forms are used for collecting population and demographic information.
    • Banks also use forms that are needed to be filled up before giving a loan to someone, when opening up a new bank account.
    • In Research, forms are used as surveys to gather feedbacks, opinions, or data in their studies.
    • Businessmen also used surveys to gather feedbacks, opinions and suggestions from their customers.
